Margam

The Dance Programme

A classical Bharatanatyam Margam — the traditional sequence of items presented at a debut recital, each building in expressive depth and complexity.

IPushpanjali
Raga: NattaiTala: Aadi

Pushpanjali, meaning "offering of flowers," is the traditional invocation that marks the beginning of a Bharatanatyam recital. Through graceful movements and rhythmic footwork, the dancer offers salutations to the divine, the guru, the accompanying musicians, and the audience. This opening piece sets a tone of humility, gratitude, and reverence, inviting blessings for the performance ahead.

IINrithyanjaliAlarippu and Devi Stuti combined
Raga: RevathyTala: Chaturasra Eka

This unique presentation blends the traditional Alarippu — a pure dance piece symbolizing the blossoming of the dancer's body and spirit — with the powerful hymn Aigiri Nandini, which praises Goddess Durga as the embodiment of strength and victory over evil. The choreography combines intricate rhythmic movements with dynamic expressions, celebrating both the discipline of Bharatanatyam and the divine feminine energy that inspires courage and devotion.

IIIKrishna Varnam
Raga: CharukeshiTala: Aadi

The Varnam, the centerpiece of a Bharatanatyam recital, showcases the dancer's technical mastery and expressive storytelling. This Krishna-themed varnam portrays the deep devotion and longing of the devotee for Lord Krishna, recalling his playful charm, divine grace, and compassionate nature. Through a seamless blend of intricate footwork and emotive abhinaya, the piece captures the joy, love, and spiritual connection shared between the devotee and the beloved deity.

IVMuruga Padam
Raga: KamachiTala: Aadi

Dedicated to Lord Muruga, the Hindu god of wisdom, courage, and victory, this energetic composition celebrates his divine valor and boundless compassion. The dance depicts Muruga's heroic qualities, his role as the protector of righteousness, and the unwavering devotion of his followers. Vibrant movements and spirited rhythms reflect the strength, grace, and youthful energy that characterize the beloved deity.

VAyyappa Sthuthi
Raga: Gowri ManohariTala: Aadi

Set to a beloved Malayalam composition, Ayyappa Sthuthi expresses heartfelt devotion to Lord Ayyappa through vivid imagery and lyrical beauty. The piece reflects on the arduous journey and the deep spiritual discipline of the pilgrimage to his forest abode in Sabarimala. Through expressive storytelling and graceful movement, the dancer conveys the unwavering spirit of surrender and the sacred bond between the devotee and the Lord of the Hills.

VIThillana
Raga: SnehajaTala: Aadi

The Thillana is the joyous finale of the Bharatanatyam repertoire, renowned for its lively rhythms, brisk footwork, and vibrant choreography. Emphasizing the beauty of pure dance, it celebrates movement, rhythm, and artistry with exuberance and precision. Bringing the recital to an energetic climax, the Thillana showcases the dancer's technical skill while leaving the audience with a sense of joy and celebration.

VIIMangalam

The recital concludes with Mangalam, a traditional benediction that offers prayers for peace, prosperity, and universal well-being. In a spirit of gratitude, the dancer pays final respects to the divine, the guru, the musicians, and the audience whose presence has made the performance complete. This closing piece signifies the end of the artistic journey while invoking blessings for all.
